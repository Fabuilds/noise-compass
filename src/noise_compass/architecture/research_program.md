## 2. Research Goals

## 3. Debate Protocol

### Step A: The Void's Challenge
The Void selects two God-Tokens (A, B) and asks:
> *"What is lost if these two collapse into each other without the distinction being maintained?"*

### Step B: Derivation-First Analysis (MANDATORY)

**B.1 — Loss Description:**
Describe the loss *without using either god-token name*. Any circular phrasing ("X is X because X") invalidates the analysis.

**B.2 — Cross-Domain Verification (`do(X~U)` Test):**
The distinction must survive maximum entropy intervention across at least 3 of 4 domains: physics, ethics, economics, biology. One sentence per domain.

**B.3 — Collapse Consequence:**
Name the specific observable failure mode. Not "structural instability" — the actual thing that breaks and what it produces.

### Step C: Candidate Verification (ONLY after B.1–B.3)
A candidate is valid if:
- **God-Token**: Eigenvalue ~1.0, non-derived ground-state pattern.
- **Gap Token**: Passes the `do(X~U)` test (survives maximum entropy intervention). Eigenvalue < 0.

### Step D: Anti-Hallucination Check
Strip all formatting from the output. If nothing substantive remains, output `NO_RESULT` and halt. Do not log empty analyses.
